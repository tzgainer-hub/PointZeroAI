The math

40 cold calls/wk × 25% conversation rate = 10 conversations × 30% book rate = 3 new discovery calls. Plus 3–5 inbound from assessment. Plus 1–2 referrals. = 7–10 discovery calls/wk. The mature-state math: at 30% close rate that's 6–10 closes/mo at $7,500–$12,500. The ramp reality: first 90 days expect 2–4/mo while the offer settles. Don't build the team around fantasy volume.

The White Coat fork (dental, medical, healthcare)

White coat practices are welcome — with a hard rule about how the build is structured. Two paths, decided before the proposal goes out.

Path B — Marketing-Only (default for white coat)

What we build: Marketing site, brand pages, services, locations, lead-capture form that hands off to the practice's existing EHR/PMS. No PHI collected, stored, transmitted, or processed by anything we build.

Sophia's job (if included): Books a consult call. Qualifies on non-medical fields only (name, contact, general reason for the consult). Routes. Does not handle insurance verification, recall, intake forms, or anything PHI-adjacent.

The hard form rule (prevents PHI drift): Path B sites do not host medical intake forms, symptom fields, insurance fields, recall forms, treatment-history fields, or free-text "describe your issue" fields. Patient-side health information goes through the practice's own scheduler, EHR, or PMS — never through a form Point Zero AI built. Lead-capture is name, contact, preferred consult time. That's it.

BAA exposure: Designed to keep PHI out of PZAI's scope entirely. Tom does not sign a BAA. The hub does not quote any feature that would require one.

Use this path: Default for every white coat prospect.

Path A — HIPAA-Touching Build (partner-led, synthetic data during build)

What gets built: Recall receptionist, insurance verifier, PHI-touching intake, patient-facing scheduling agents.

The hard rule, in order:

  1. HIPAA-capable partner contracts with the practice and signs the BAA before any build work starts.
  2. Point Zero AI builds and tests using synthetic / placeholder data only. No PHI is created, received, transmitted, accessed, or stored by PZAI at any point.
  3. At launch, the partner activates the system with real patient data under the BAA. PZAI hands over admin access and steps fully out of the PHI chain.
  4. Post-launch, the partner owns operations, monitoring, breach response, and BAA lifecycle. PZAI does not access, monitor, or troubleshoot anything PHI-touching.

Why the synthetic-data rule: HHS treats anyone who creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI on behalf of a covered entity as a Business Associate — even before a formal handoff. Building with synthetic data keeps PZAI out of that definition entirely. Tom never holds a BAA. PZAI never holds a BAA.

Pre-condition: A HIPAA-capable partner must be on the bench AND the BAA between the practice and the partner must be signed before any build kickoff. If either is missing, Path A can be quoted but not built. Closer documents the dependency in the proposal.

Use this path: Only when the prospect explicitly demands HIPAA features and the partner is locked.

What never happens

Tom does not sign a BAA. Point Zero AI does not sign a BAA. If a closer cannot route the build to either Path B or a partner-led Path A, the deal walks. That trade is not on the table.

  5. Email-only agents are zero-friction add-ons. Quote Follow-Up, Annual Service, Reactivation — deploy fast. Use these as easy-yes upsells for clients who already have a build live.

Section 05

Prospect Tiers.

Not every business is a fit. The tier system tells you who to spend time on, who to disqualify fast, and which package to pitch.

The qualifying gate (all tiers must pass)

  • Average ticket size $1,500+
  • Emergency or after-hours component to the business (HVAC, plumbing, water damage, electrical, locksmith, towing, vet, etc.)
  • Owner answers their own phone (small enough to be reachable; not enterprise)
  • Annual revenue $500K–$5M (enough cash flow to afford us, small enough to need us)
  • Already paying for Google Ads (proves they invest in lead-gen)

If a prospect fails any of these, they're a Disqualify. Don't waste a slot on them. Refer them to a generic web shop and move on.

The four tiers

Tier S — Full Response System
Highest Value

Profile: No website at all, OR site is broken/parked, OR hasn't materially changed in 5+ years. Often a Facebook page or a Wix template from 2016 with default placeholder text still on it.

Pitch: Full Response System — $12,500 setup + partner Care plan.

Why they're gold: No incumbent web vendor to compete with. Owner already knows the site is embarrassing. Cleanest sales conversation we have.

Tier A — Smart Site + Sophia (default)
Strong Fit

Profile: Functional but stale website (3–5 years old). Mobile-responsive but dated. No live chat, no AI, no online booking. Site works but is forgettable.

Pitch: Smart Site + Sophia — $7,500 setup + partner Care plan. This is the bread-and-butter offer.

Why they're solid: Owner has invested in the channel before, so they value it. Refresh + Sophia feels like an upgrade, not starting over.

Tier B — Standalone Sophia
Easy Add-On

Profile: Modern, well-maintained site (built within last 2 years). No voice AI. Owner happy with site, frustrated by missed calls.

Pitch: Sophia Voice Agent standalone — $2,500 setup + partner Care plan. Open the door to upgrade fees in months 4–6.

Why volume matters here: Quicker close (single decision, lower setup), but lower per-deal revenue. Goal is upgrade expansion once they trust us.

Tier C — Likely Not a Fit
Disqualify Fast

Profile: Already has live chat, AI receptionist, modern booking widgets. Has a tech vendor relationship. Polished, well-funded.

Pitch: None on a cold call. If they request a discovery, qualify hard for what their current system doesn't do (lead qualification gaps, follow-up gaps, missing chat agent, etc.) and pitch the standalone agent that closes the gap.

Why we don't chase them: They're a long sale, low close rate, and the existing vendor is a wall.

Where to find Tier S

Use Google Maps as the primary source. Search "[trade] near [city]." Look for businesses with real reviews, real phone, but no website link in the listing — or a website link that returns broken. Skip Chamber of Commerce CSVs — they're polluted with miscategorized businesses. Skip SEO doorway pages.

Section 06

Cold Outreach.

The phone is the primary channel. Email is the follow-up. Texting is for already-engaged prospects only. Goal of every cold call is one thing: book a 30-minute discovery call.

The cold call script

Cold call · 60 seconds Hi, is this [Owner Name]? — This is [Rep Name] from Point Zero AI. I know you weren't expecting my call so I'll be quick.

I work with [trade] businesses in [city], and the reason I'm calling specifically is — I noticed your business comes up on Google for [their service] but I don't see a 24/7 way for someone to reach you when you're on a job or after hours.

We build smart response systems for businesses like yours — the site, plus a voice agent named Sophia that answers, qualifies the lead, and books the job into your calendar, even at 2am on a Sunday.

Can I show you a 5-minute demo on Tuesday or Wednesday? Whether it's a fit or not, you'll know in five minutes.

The structure (memorize this, not the words)

  • Permission — "I know you weren't expecting my call." Disarms.
  • Specificity — their trade, their city, what you noticed. Proves you researched.
  • The problem — missed after-hours calls. Universally felt.
  • The product — one sentence. Not a pitch.
  • The next step — a 5-minute demo before any pricing. The demo is what earns the price conversation, not the cold call.
  • The ask — specific day. Not "sometime next week."

The voicemail script

Voicemail · 18 seconds Hi [Owner Name], this is [Rep Name] from Point Zero AI. I work with [trade] businesses on the after-hours call problem. We build smart response systems — a voice agent that answers, qualifies, and books jobs 24/7.

I'll send you a quick email with a link to a 5-minute demo. If it looks interesting, my number is [Phone]. Have a good one.

The five stages

They take the assessment

Stage 2 · Booking
Discovery call books

Post-call decision

Within 30 min of hangup: complete Sections J–M of the intake form. Tier confirmation, fit score, recommended package, send-by date for proposal. Send proposal same-day for any "Yes" or "Soft yes."

Section 08

Discovery Questions.

The 14 questions the assessment asks (which we already have answers to), plus the questions to ask live during the discovery call.

What the assessment asks (you have these answers before the call)

Your Business

  • What industry are you in?
  • How many people work in your business, including you?
  • How long have you been in business?

Your Time

  • How many hours per week are you losing to tasks you know you shouldn't be doing yourself?
  • Which tasks eat most of your time?
  • Where does work pile up waiting on you specifically?

Your Leads & Clients

  • When a new lead or inquiry comes in after hours or on weekends, what happens?
  • When a prospect doesn't close on the first conversation, how do you follow up?
  • How many past clients haven't heard from you in 6+ months?
  • How does your business get reviews, referrals, or testimonials?

Your Digital Presence

  • When was the last time your website had a significant upgrade?
  • Does your website generate leads on its own, or do all your new clients come from referrals?

Your Systems & Goals

  • How do you currently handle client communication and follow-up?
  • What are your top goals over the next 12 months?

What to ask live (during the discovery call)

Open & Confirm (5 min)

Lead with what you already know.

  • "I pulled up your site this morning. [Last meaningful change date]. Mobile speed [score]. [Missing capability]. That's actually pretty common for [industry] your size — want to make sure I'm seeing it right before I tell you what I think."
  • "What's the actual experience right now when a customer tries to reach you on a Saturday?"

Quantify the Pain (10 min)

  • "What's your average ticket or job size?"
  • "Roughly how many leads come in per month?"
  • "What % of those come in after hours or on weekends?"
  • "What's your rough close rate on a qualified lead?"
  • "How much do you spend on Google Ads per month right now?"
  • "If you had to guess — how many leads a week do you think slip through?"
Math to do live

monthly leads × after-hours % × ticket size × close rate × 12 = annual leak. Say the number out loud. "So just from the after-hours problem, you're leaving roughly $[X] on the table every year."

Decision & Timeline (5 min)

  • "If we found something that solved this, are you the person who decides? Or anyone else?"
  • "On a scale of 1–10, how urgent is fixing this for you?"
  • "What changes if you don't fix this in the next 90 days?"
  • "Have you tried to fix this before? What happened?"

Stack & Vendors (5 min)

  • "Who built your current website? Are they still maintaining it?"
  • "What CRM — if any — are you using?"
  • "Who answers your phone right now during the day? Who answers at 8pm on a Sunday?"
  • "Anything we'd be replacing or working alongside?"

Vision Question (5 min)

The one question that closes the call.

If we close these gaps and 12 months from now this is working — what does that look like for you?

Whatever they say, write it down verbatim. Use those exact words in the proposal.

Section 09

Demo Flow.

The demo is 5 minutes. Three things happen: they hear Sophia, they see the call summary, they see the booked job in the calendar. Anything more is a distraction.

The 5-minute demo script

0:00 — 0:30
Set the stage

"What you're about to see is a call I'm going to make right now to a number we set up for [their trade]. I'm going to pretend I'm a customer with [their typical emergency]. Watch what Sophia does."

0:30 — 2:30
Make the call live

Call the demo Sophia number on speaker. Let them hear the full conversation: greeting → qualifying questions → booking → confirmation. Don't narrate. Let it play.

2:30 — 3:30
Show the call summary

Within 60 seconds of hangup, the call summary lands as a text + email. Show your screen: the transcript, the captured customer info, the qualified problem, the booked time. "This is what you'd get on your phone right now."

3:30 — 4:30
Show the calendar

Open the calendar. The job is on it. "She handed off the booking to your scheduler. You did nothing. The customer got a clear, professional answer at 11pm on a Sunday — and you'd already won the job by morning."

4:30 — 5:00
The close question

"If Sophia ran on your phone tonight, what's the first kind of call you'd want her to handle?"

Why this demo wins

  • Live, not recorded. Anything can be faked in a video. A live call cannot.
  • It's their problem. The fake customer scenario you act out is the call they themselves miss most often.
  • The handoff is the magic. Most prospects expect the AI to be impressive on the call. They're not expecting the call summary + booked calendar 60 seconds later. That's where they lean forward.

What NOT to demo

Avoid

Don't show the admin dashboard. Don't show the prompt configuration. Don't show the analytics. They don't care about how Sophia is built — they care about what she does. Show outputs only.

The post-demo question

After they react, ask one of these (whichever fits the moment):

  • "What's your gut reaction?"
  • "Could you see this running on your line tonight?"
  • "What would you want her to handle differently for [their trade]?"

Their answer tells you whether to send a proposal today or schedule a follow-up.

Section 10

Objection Handlers.

Eleven objections cover 95% of pushback. Memorize the structure: acknowledge → reframe → ask. Never argue.

01 · The web guy
My web guy can probably just add a chatbot for me.

"Totally fair question. Here's the difference: a chatbot is a feature. A smart response system is a managed service. We design and build it. Then a dedicated support partner runs it — the partner-managed line, the voice tuning, the monthly call review, the troubleshooting at 11pm on a Sunday. Your web guy installs a chatbot and moves on to the next site. We're not here for the install. We're here for the system that keeps working. Does that make sense?"

02 · Price
$7,500 is more than I expected.

"Fair. Let me ask — what's one of your typical jobs worth? [They answer.] So we're really asking whether the system books you [N] of those jobs over its first year that you would have missed otherwise. The math isn't whether $7,500 is a lot of money — it's whether the response system will recover that in jobs you're already losing right now. Want me to walk you through what your annual leak looks like?"

03 · Already have someone
I already have an answering service.

"Good — that means you've already done the math that after-hours coverage matters. Quick question: does your current service book the appointment into your calendar, or do they take a message and you call back? — Right. Sophia closes the loop. She doesn't take a message; she books the job. The customer never has to wait for a callback. Want to see the difference on a 5-minute demo?"

04 · Wait and see
Let me think about it.

"Of course. Can I ask — what specifically do you need to think about? Is it the price, the technology, or something about how it'd fit your business? — That way I can either give you the answer right now or get out of your way and let you decide."

05 · Need a partner
I need to talk to my partner / spouse / accountant.

"Smart. Two things: what's the one question they're going to ask that you don't have an answer to right now? — Let me answer it now so you can have a productive conversation with them. And second — what's a good day this week or next for the three of us to do a quick 15-minute call? Often it's faster if I can answer their questions directly."

06 · Already have a website
My website is fine.

"Good — if it works for you, it works. Sophia plugs into any existing site, so we don't need to touch yours. The right move there is Sophia standalone — $2,500 to set her up on your line, plus the partner Care plan for ongoing. You keep your site. Want to see how she'd run on your number?"

07 · AI fear
My customers don't want to talk to a robot.

"Fair concern. The voice is natural and professional, and Sophia is configured to be transparent and focused on one thing — getting the qualified call booked. Here's the part that matters: a customer calling at 11pm gets a real conversation that books their job, OR they get voicemail, OR they call your competitor. Which one do you actually want them to experience?"

08 · Proof
How do I know this actually works?

"Best way is to hear it yourself — that's why the demo is 5 minutes and live, not a video. Beyond that, we guarantee the system: Sophia launches as scoped, answers calls, qualifies, summarizes, and books according to the approved workflow. If the launch is materially broken in the first 30 days, we fix it or refund the setup minus hard costs. We don't guarantee how many jobs come in — that depends on your call volume and market — but we do guarantee the system works as designed."

09 · HIPAA / Compliance
What about HIPAA / patient privacy?

"Important question, and we have a clean answer. We give every white coat practice two paths. The default is a marketing-only build — great site, lead capture for new patients, but no patient data flowing through anything we build. That path is designed to keep PHI out of our scope entirely. If you want recall, insurance verification, or PHI-touching workflows, that goes through our HIPAA-capable support partner who contracts directly with you under a signed BAA before any PHI-touching work begins. Point Zero AI doesn't sign BAAs and doesn't access patient data. The partner owns the compliance side. Which sounds closer to what you actually need?"

10 · Tech overwhelm
I'm not tech savvy — this sounds complicated.

"That's actually who we built this for. You don't configure anything. You don't log into a dashboard. You give us your phone, your calendar, and the questions you want her to ask — we set it up, then your support partner keeps it running. The only time you interact with Sophia is when you read the call summary on your phone. If you can read a text message, you can run this."

11 · Why a partner for support?
Why isn't Point Zero AI doing the monthly support directly?

"Good question. We design, build, and launch the response system — that's the work that takes strategy and judgment. Day-to-day support, monitoring, and edits go to our implementation support partner who's set up to do that work at scale. You get faster response times, clearer scope, and a phone number that's actually answered. We stay involved on strategic upgrades and the quarterly review. It's the same way good architects work with general contractors after the building is done. Different people, different specialty."

The universal frame

When stuck

"That's a fair concern. Let me ask you something — what would have to be true for this to make sense for [Business Name]?" — The answer they give you is the actual objection. Solve that one.

Section 11

Proposal & Close.

Send the proposal within 24 hours of the discovery call. The faster it arrives, the higher the close rate.

The Two Tracks — routing decision before the proposal goes out

Every closed deal routes to one of two tracks. The decision is made during the close call, before the proposal is drafted, because the contract language differs.

Partner-Served Track  ·  default

Who it's for: 95%+ of clients. The standard model.

How it works: Point Zero AI designs, builds, and launches. At launch, the client signs a Care plan directly with our implementation support partner. Partner handles all ongoing support, monthly edits, agent QA, troubleshooting. Partner pays Point Zero AI a 15–20% referral/oversight slice on the monthly. Tom is out of the support chair.

Contract structure: Two contracts. (1) PZAI build agreement (setup fee, scope, launch terms). (2) Partner Care agreement (monthly support, signed directly with partner). Closer brings both to the close call.

Tom-Direct Track  ·  capped at 10 active clients, no exceptions

Who it's for: Strategic accounts only — personal connections, high-leverage referral sources, or marquee case-study candidates.

The hard cap: Maximum 10 active Tom-Direct clients at any time. When the slot count is full, every new client routes to Partner-Served by default. If a closer can't tell you which of the 10 a new prospect would replace, the prospect goes to Partner.

The purpose: These 10 are referral engines. Tom keeps the relationship close so he can ask for case studies, intros, and warm referrals into the partner pipeline. Tom is the support contact for these 10 only.

How to use the slot: Closer flags a Tom-Direct candidate before quoting. Tom approves yes/no. If approved, contract is single-stream: PZAI build + PZAI monthly Care, billed by PZAI, served by Tom.

The trap to avoid: Tom-Direct is not the "I like this client" track. It's the "this client will refer me 5 more" track. Treat the 10 slots like a portfolio, not a friend list.

Default routing rule

When in doubt, route to Partner-Served. It is structurally easier to upgrade a Partner client to Tom-Direct than to push a Tom-Direct client back to Partner. Default is Partner. Exception is Tom.

The proposal template

Three pages, plain language, no jargon. Mirrors the prospect's verbatim language from the discovery call (Section M of the intake form).

Page 1 — What you said

Bullet points of THEIR words. "You said you're losing roughly $[X] a year to after-hours leads." "You said your goal is to [their stated 12-month goal]." "You said your current site is [their description]." This page proves we listened.

Page 2 — What we recommend

The package + agents + setup price. Itemized but simple. One line of timeline ("Live in 2–4 weeks"). One line of guarantee ("30-day satisfaction guarantee on the build"). Explicit ongoing-support clause: "After launch, ongoing edits, monitoring, and changes are handled by our implementation support partner under a separate Care plan ($497–$1,497/mo) signed directly with the partner. Point Zero AI is not the day-to-day support provider." (For Tom-Direct clients only, swap the clause to "Point Zero AI handles ongoing support directly under our Care plan, $497–$1,497/mo.")

Page 3 — What happens next

Three steps. Sign the build agreement (link). Sign the partner Care plan (separate link — Partner-Served only). Onboarding kickoff call (Calendly). Launch (auto-scheduled date). Total length: under 120 words. Make it feel inevitable.

Section 12

The Red Zone.

What Point Zero AI does not do. Read this before every quote. The deals that go sideways start when a closer agrees to something on this list because it felt small in the moment.

The frame

This is a family-option business. Every "yes" outside the boundary is a future support call, lawsuit, or 9pm phone ring that lands on Tom or his sons. The Red Zone protects the asset.

Compliance & liability

  • Tom does not sign BAAs. Point Zero AI does not sign BAAs. Ever.
  • No PHI through anything we build on Path B (default white coat path). The marketing site does not collect, store, transmit, or process patient health information.
  • Path A (HIPAA-touching) requires a HIPAA-capable partner on the bench. If no partner is locked, Path A can be sold but not built. Closer documents the dependency in the proposal. No closer agrees to build PHI features without partner BAA in place.
  • No legal advice agents. Including: contract review, document drafting agents that produce legal output, anything an unlicensed practice of law claim could attach to.
  • No financial advice agents. Including: investment recommendation agents, robo-advisor chat agents, securities-adjacent intake.
  • No medical diagnosis or triage agents. Including: symptom-checker chat, "is this an emergency" routing, diagnosis-adjacent intake.

Operational compliance

  • No outbound AI voice or SMS without TCPA review. Follow-Up Agent, Quote Follow-Up Caller, or any agent that initiates contact requires documented prior consent, opt-out language, and legal/TCPA review on the client side. The FCC has confirmed AI-generated voices in robocalls fall under TCPA — treat that as the floor.
  • No call recording, transcription, or summary storage without consent. Sophia produces transcripts and summaries by design. Every client deployment requires approved consent language on the inbound greeting, defined retention rules, and the client's written approval before recordings or transcripts are stored.
  • No payment-card or bank-credential collection through any agent we build. If the client wants payments, we route to an approved PCI-compliant provider (Stripe Checkout, etc.) — the agent never collects card numbers, CVV, or banking info.
  • No emergency, safety, medical, legal, or hazard advice from any agent. If a caller describes immediate danger, the agent's job is to route to 911 or the client's pre-approved emergency instruction. Sophia is not a triage system. Especially relevant for HVAC, electrical, water damage, locksmith, towing, vet.
  • No eligibility or qualification decisions for regulated categories. No agent makes credit, housing, employment, insurance, healthcare, lending, or benefits eligibility calls. Capture intent, route to a human.
  • No client passwords sent by email or stored in personal accounts. All credentials live in approved shared credential tools (1Password, Bitwarden, etc.). Admin access is documented, transferred at launch, and removed from PZAI staff at handoff.

Pricing floors

  • No custom build under $15,000. Custom = anything outside the four published packages. Below $15k, custom work is a margin trap and a scope-creep magnet.
  • No Sophia-only at $297/mo with no setup. The cheap monthly trains the wrong client and creates support volume Tom doesn't own. Standalone Sophia is $2,500 setup + partner Care.
  • No discount of build setup in exchange for a larger Care plan. Care belongs to the partner. Trading PZAI revenue for partner revenue is bad math for everyone.
  • No "free month" of Care. The first month of partner Care is due at launch. Build setup includes launch QA only, not free Care months.
  • No setup fee waived. If a prospect can't pay the setup, they can't afford the system to support them after.

Promises we never make

  • No 24/7 personal availability from Tom. Even Tom-Direct clients get business-hours support windows, not "call whenever."
  • No lifetime support guarantee. Care is month-to-month after the initial 90 days. Either side can exit with 30 days' notice.
  • No "we'll fix anything" scope. The Care plan defines what's in scope. Out-of-scope work is quoted separately as upgrade fees (see Section 03).
  • No uncapped change requests. Each Care tier specifies a change-request volume. Beyond that = upgrade fee.
  • No timeline guarantees on prospect-supplied content. If the client doesn't deliver photos, copy, or service info on schedule, the build clock pauses.

Language we never use

  • "The customer doesn't know it's AI." Lawsuit bait. Use: "Sophia sounds natural and is configured to be transparent and professional."
  • "We sign a BAA." We do not. Use: "Our HIPAA-capable support partner signs the BAA directly with the practice before any PHI-touching work begins."
  • "HIPAA-certified" anything. No magic certification exists. Use "HIPAA-capable" or "operating under a signed BAA and compliance framework."
  • "Zero HIPAA exposure." Never promise an absolute. Use "Path B is designed to keep PHI out of anything Point Zero AI builds."
  • "Guaranteed compliance." Compliance is a partner deliverable, not a PZAI promise.
  • "We'll figure it out." If we can't scope it on the call, it goes in writing or doesn't go at all.
  • "AI agents." Use "smart response system" with prospects. AI is a feature; the response is the product.
  • "Unlimited." Nothing is unlimited. Every Care tier has change volume bands.

Industries we say no to

  • Cannabis & CBD retail — payment processing, advertising, and compliance complications outweigh the deal value.
  • Adult / gambling / firearms direct-sale — ad-platform restrictions choke lead-gen ROI and our agent providers don't support these verticals.
  • MLM / network marketing recruitment — reputational risk, compliance gray zones.
  • Crypto / DeFi / token sales — regulatory risk, fraud-adjacent industry-wide.
  • Anyone Tom or the team has a direct competitive interest with — flag to Tom before quoting.

Client behaviors that disqualify

  • "Can you call me at 9pm tonight to discuss?" — Wrong client expectation. Reframe or walk.
  • "Just send me a one-pager and I'll think about it." — Refuses the discovery call. Not a real prospect.
  • "My nephew can probably do this for cheaper." — Will be a constant comparison client. Walk.
  • "I'll pay you a percentage of the leads it brings in." — Performance-pay deals are always lopsided. We don't do them.
  • "Can you sign an NDA before we talk?" — Cold prospects asking for NDAs are time sinks. Decline.
When in doubt

If a request from a prospect makes you reach for a creative workaround — that's the moment to check the Red Zone. The boundary exists precisely because reasonable-sounding asks become bad businesses one yes at a time.
