Section 01

Welcome, Partner.

This hub is for our implementation support partner. It is the operating doc — what you own, what we own, how clients move from build to ongoing service, what's in scope, and what isn't. If a question comes up, the answer is here.

What this partnership is

Point Zero AI designs, builds, and launches smart response systems for service businesses. Once a system goes live, you take over the ongoing relationship — monthly support, edits, monitoring, agent QA, integrations maintenance. Clients sign a Care plan directly with you. You bill the client. You pay Point Zero AI a 15–20% referral/oversight slice on the monthly recurring revenue.

What this partnership is not

  • Not a referral firehose. We don't dump every lead. We screen, build, and hand over launched, working systems.
  • Not white-label. Clients know Point Zero AI built the system and you support it. Two named brands. Clean handoff.
  • Not subcontracting. The client signs with you directly. Your contract. Your billing. Your accountability.
  • Not unlimited. Care plans have defined scope and change-volume bands. Out-of-scope work routes back to PZAI as upgrade quotes.

Who's responsible for what (one-line version)

The whole model in one sentence

"Point Zero AI builds and launches the system. The partner runs and maintains it. The client signs two contracts — one for the build (PZAI), one for ongoing care (partner)."

Why this works

  • For the client: Specialty matters. The team that designs the system isn't the right team to fix buttons at 8pm. You are.
  • For you: Recurring revenue without sales overhead. We bring the clients to you with systems already configured.
  • For Point Zero AI: Clean separation of build and run. We stay focused on what we're good at; you do the same.
Section 02

The Partnership Model.

How money, contracts, and accountability flow.

Contract structure

Every Partner-Served client signs two separate contracts:

Build Agreement — with Point Zero AI
  • Setup fee ($3,500–$15,000+ depending on package)
  • Build scope, timeline, deliverables
  • 30-day satisfaction guarantee on the launched build
  • Build clock and asset-deadline rules
  • Ends at: launch + partner handoff
Care Agreement — with Partner
  • Monthly fee ($497–$1,497 by tier)
  • Defined support scope (Section 03)
  • Change-request volume bands
  • Month-to-month after initial 90-day term
  • 30-day cancellation notice

The PZAI slice on Care

Partner pays Point Zero AI a percentage of every Care plan's monthly recurring revenue. Default tier: 15% on Basic ($497/mo), 18% on Growth ($997/mo), 20% on Pro ($1,497/mo). Adjustable based on partner volume and tenure (negotiated in the partner agreement).

Section 03

Support Scope.

What's included in a Care plan, in writing. If the client asks for something outside this list, it's an upgrade — quoted by Point Zero AI.

What every Care plan includes (regardless of tier)

  • Sophia agent monitoring. Spot-check call logs at minimum cadence by tier (Section 04).
  • System uptime monitoring. Alerts if site, Sophia, or critical integrations go down.
  • Calendar / CRM sync health. Ensure bookings land where they should.
  • Email + SMS confirmation delivery. Ensure customer-facing notifications keep firing.
  • Bug fixes & small content updates. Within the change-volume band for the tier.
  • Monthly performance report to the client.
  • Business-hours support response window (defined per tier).
  • Quarterly handover sync with PZAI — surfacing patterns, upgrade opportunities, escalations.

What's not in scope (upgrade quotes go to PZAI)

  • New site sections, new pages, redesigns
  • New AI agents or major Sophia retraining (vs. tuning)
  • New CRM platforms or integrations
  • Multi-location expansion
  • Net-new automations / workflows
  • Custom reporting beyond monthly digest
  • Migration to different hosting / platforms
  • SEO content campaigns / blog production
  • Anything the client describes as "a new project"
The simple test

If a request would have been quoted as an Upgrade Fee in the original Sales Hub (Section 03), it's an upgrade — not a Care request. Send it back to PZAI for scoping.

Quick decision tree

Section 06

Receiving a New Client.

The handoff process from PZAI build team to partner. Six steps, all happen within 7 days of launch.

Day -7 (pre-launch)
Pre-handoff briefing

PZAI build lead schedules a 15-min briefing with partner. Shares: client context, Sophia config nuances, integrations status, anticipated quirks. Partner asks questions; nothing is in writing yet but partner understands what's coming.

Launch day
Launch announcement

Partner is cc'd on PZAI's launch communication to the client. Partner does not yet engage — PZAI is still primary.

Launch + 3 days
Handoff package delivered

PZAI delivers the full handoff package (build artifacts + operating docs) to /clients/[business-name]/handoff/ in shared drive. Partner reviews. Flags anything missing within 24 hours.

Launch + 5 days
Partner kickoff meeting

30 min. Builder + partner + Tom (oversight). Walk-through of handoff package + live demo + Q&A. Partner confirms takeover date.

Launch + 7 days
Client introduction email

Builder sends the introduction email (template in Section 07), cc'ing partner. This is when the client formally learns who their day-to-day contact is going forward.

Launch + 14 days
First partner check-in with client

Partner schedules a 20-min intro call with the client. Goal: confirm partner is responsive, surface any early issues, set expectation on monthly reports and response times.

What partner receives in the handoff package

Build artifacts

  • Site source / CMS access (admin)
  • Sophia configuration export
  • Twilio number + account access (admin)
  • Calendar / CRM credentials
  • GHL workflow exports (default 6)
  • Domain / DNS records

Operating docs

  • Client-specific Sophia notes (voice tuning, edge cases)
  • Service-area + pricing reference
  • Stakeholder map (decision-maker, payer, tester)
  • Active integrations list
  • Known quirks discovered during build
  • 30-day QC plan handed off to partner
If the handoff package is incomplete

If something is missing, flag it within 24 hours of receipt. Don't assume "we'll figure it out later." Missing handoff items at Day 30 become support tickets the partner can't answer.

• If you want to expand the system — new pages, new agents, multi-location — I'll loop in Point Zero AI for the build side.

Template: Monthly performance report

Sent by partner to client on the 5th of each month, covering the prior month. Partner-branded but follows this structure:

  • Headline numbers: total calls, booked jobs, missed calls, after-hours volume
  • Sophia performance: avg call length, qualification accuracy, booking rate
  • System health: uptime %, integration status, any incidents
  • Changes made this month: what partner did (within Care scope)
  • Recommendations: 1–3 observations or upgrade suggestions (route upgrades to PZAI)
  • Next month focus: 1 thing partner is watching

Tone & voice

  • Professional but warm. Service-business owners trust people, not departments.
  • Direct, not jargon-heavy. "Sophia missed 3 calls last week" beats "agent latency anomaly."
  • No promises about outcomes (lead volume, conversion). Speak only to what partner controls.
  • Refer up to PZAI on any strategic question. Don't freelance the answer.

Special rules

  • For P1: follow up with text to Tom's mobile if no email reply within 1 hour during business hours

Section 10

White Coat & BAA Flow.

For dental, medical, and healthcare clients on Path A (HIPAA-touching builds). Path B clients are zero-PHI — this section doesn't apply to them.

The hard rule, restated

Tom does not sign BAAs. Point Zero AI does not sign BAAs.

For any HIPAA-touching build, the partner signs the Business Associate Agreement and assumes the compliance role. PZAI builds the system to your compliance specifications. The contract structure exists to keep PZAI out of the BAA chain entirely.

Path A workflow

Sales call
Closer flags Path A intent

If a healthcare prospect specifically wants HIPAA-touching features, closer notes Path A in the proposal. PZAI sales confirms with partner that you have BAA capacity for this client.

Pre-build
Partner signs BAA with client

Before any PHI-touching build starts, partner and client execute the BAA. PZAI does not start building until the BAA is in place. This is a hard gate, not a paperwork formality.

Build phase
PZAI builds to compliance spec

PZAI builds the system following the partner's HIPAA-compliant infrastructure requirements: encrypted data at rest, encrypted in transit, access logging, audit trails, segregated environments, etc.

Pre-launch
Compliance review

Partner runs a compliance review of the built system. Sign-off is a precondition for launch.

Launch + handoff
Partner takes operational ownership

Partner runs the live system under the BAA. PZAI transfers all PHI-touching access and credentials to partner. PZAI staff retain no access to PHI. Partner-only.

Ongoing
Annual BAA renewal & audit

Partner manages the BAA lifecycle: renewals, amendments, breach notifications, periodic compliance audits. PZAI is not a party to any of this.

What PZAI commits to on Path A

  • Build to partner's stated infrastructure requirements
  • Document data flows and any PHI touchpoints in the build
  • Hand over all access and credentials at launch
  • Retain no PHI access post-launch
  • Notify partner of any planned upgrades or changes that would touch PHI

What partner commits to on Path A

  • Sign the BAA with the client before build starts
  • Provide a written compliance spec for the build
  • Run the compliance review pre-launch
  • Operate the live system under the BAA, including breach response
  • Carry appropriate professional liability / cyber insurance
  • Notify PZAI of any compliance incident affecting build artifacts
If partner can't sign a BAA

If partner does not have BAA capacity (no compliance program, no insurance, no infrastructure), Path A clients cannot be sold — only Path B (marketing-only, zero PHI). PZAI sales is informed of partner's BAA capacity at all times. "We can do HIPAA" is a binary capability, not a maybe.

Section 11

The Red Zone (Partner Edition).

What partner does not do, even if a client asks. The boundaries that keep the partnership clean.

Out of scope, period

  • No PHI handling on Path B clients. If a Path B client asks for a PHI feature, refer back to PZAI — not as an upgrade quote, but as a path-conversion conversation.
  • No legal / financial / medical advice agents. Same red lines as PZAI's Sales Hub Section 11.
  • No solicitation of clients away from the PZAI ecosystem. Partner does not pitch competing services to PZAI-originated clients.
  • No subcontracting without PZAI awareness. If partner uses subcontractors for support work, PZAI is informed and contractors honor the same rules.
  • No undercutting upgrades. If a client asks for new pages or agents, partner refers to PZAI — doesn't quietly build them at a discount.
  • No after-hours / 24/7 availability promises beyond what's in the Care plan.

Language partner doesn't use with clients

  • "I'll talk to Tom about it." → "I'll bring this back to Point Zero AI for a scope and quote."
  • "We can do that for free." → "That's outside the Care plan, but I can get you a quick scope from PZAI."
  • "Just text me." → "Email's the best way to reach me — I respond within [X business hours]."
  • "I'll handle the legal side." → never. Refer to client's own counsel.

Things partner doesn't volunteer

  • Pricing on builds, agents, or upgrades. That's PZAI's lane.
  • Opinions on PZAI's strategy or roadmap. Surface observations in monthly reports; not in client conversations.
  • Discount offers or extensions. All commercial terms come from PZAI.
When in doubt

"I'd like to confirm the right answer with Point Zero AI — I'll come back to you within one business day." No client expects an instant answer to every question. Buying time to align is professional, not weak.

Section 12

Partner Commitments.

What partner agrees to in exchange for the steady stream of launched clients and the recurring revenue. The non-financial side of the partner agreement.

Operational commitments

  • Business entity — partner operates as a registered business entity (LLC, S-Corp, or similar) with EIN. No sole-proprietor handshake deals.
  • Professional email + ticket system — support@ address with a real ticket trail, not a personal Gmail.
  • Defined response time — matching the tier promise. Documented and tracked.
  • Direct billing capability — partner can bill clients on auto-pay (Stripe, ACH, or similar). No "PZAI bills and I take a cut" inversions.
  • Insurance — professional liability, cyber liability (especially for Path A), errors & omissions. Levels specified in partner agreement.
  • Documentation discipline — every client interaction is logged. Every change is documented. No tribal-knowledge support.
  • Monthly reporting on time — due by the 10th, every month. (Section 09.)

Brand & relationship commitments

  • Honor the brand split — clients know PZAI built the system and partner supports it. Don't pretend otherwise in either direction.
  • No client poaching — partner does not solicit PZAI-originated clients to migrate to partner-only services.
  • No competing offers — partner does not pitch competitive build services to PZAI clients during the partnership.
  • Public references — partner does not publicly claim to have built systems they only support. Partner can publicly reference the partnership with PZAI's permission.
  • Confidentiality — client data, PZAI's hubs, internal pricing structures, and partner agreements are confidential.

Quality commitments

  • Care plan delivery — partner delivers what each Care tier promises. Failure rates trigger partnership review.
  • Client retention — partner aims for 12+ month average client retention. Below 8 months without external cause is a partnership-health conversation.
  • Client satisfaction — partner runs a satisfaction check (NPS or equivalent) at month 3 and month 12. Shared in monthly reports.

What PZAI commits to partner

  • Bring launched, working systems — not half-baked builds
  • Honor the upgrade-quote pipeline — route upgrade work back through partner first
  • Pay slice on time, every month
  • Provide clear and accurate handoff packages
  • Loop partner in on roadmap changes that affect support
  • Honor the partnership in client conversations — never bypass partner for direct support work outside agreed exceptions
Section 13

Exit / Replacement Process.

Partnerships end. The question is whether they end cleanly. This section is what happens if either side decides to part ways — or if performance triggers a replacement.

Voluntary partnership exit

Either side can exit the partnership with 90 days' written notice. During the 90 days:

  • No new clients are routed to the exiting partner
  • Active clients continue to be served at full Care levels
  • PZAI identifies and onboards a replacement partner (if applicable)
  • Knowledge transfer plan is built and executed before the 90 days end
  • Slice payments continue through the notice period for clients still under partner's Care

Performance-triggered replacement

PZAI may initiate a partnership-health review if any of these patterns emerge:

  • Two consecutive months of missed monthly reports without notice
  • Three or more [P1] escalations from a single partner in a quarter that should have been handled directly
  • Sustained client complaint pattern (3+ clients in a quarter raising response-time or quality issues)
  • Slice payments late by more than 30 days
  • Discovery of any Red Zone violation (Section 11)
  • Client churn rate > 20% over a rolling 6 months without external cause

Partnership-health review is a conversation, not an automatic termination. The goal is to fix what's fixable. If unfixable, the 90-day exit clock starts.

Client transition to a new partner

When a client moves from one partner to another (whether by exit or replacement):

Day 0
PZAI informs client of transition

PZAI sends client a clear, professional explanation of the partner change. Names the new partner. Assures continuity.

Day 0–14
Outgoing partner prepares handoff

Outgoing partner compiles: client-specific notes, all changes made under their tenure, open issues, integration credentials. Stored in the client's /handoff/ folder, partner subfolder.

Day 14–21
New partner onboards on the client

Same handoff process as Section 06, but with outgoing partner instead of PZAI builder as the briefing source.

Day 21
Client introduction to new partner

PZAI sends transition email. Client now has new partner's contact info. Old partner steps back.

Day 21–30
Stability period

New partner runs the client's Care while having access to outgoing partner for clarification questions. After Day 30, transition is complete.

What partner is owed at exit

  • All slice payments due through the notice period
  • 30-day final reconciliation period after notice ends, for any payment trailing
  • Reasonable cooperation from PZAI on the transition

What partner owes at exit

  • Continued service quality through the notice period (no coast mode)
  • Complete handoff documentation for every active client
  • Return of credentials, access, and client data per the partner agreement
  • Confidentiality continues post-exit
  • Non-solicit of PZAI clients per the partner agreement (typically 12 months)
The why behind the structure

The exit process is not designed to make partnerships hard to end. It's designed to make them end well, so clients don't pay the price for the partnership change. A clean exit is its own reference for future partnerships.
