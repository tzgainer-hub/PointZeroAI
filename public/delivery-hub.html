Section 01

From Signed Deal to Launched System.

This hub is for the builder. It picks up where the Sales Hub ends — the moment a contract is signed — and walks the client all the way to a partner-handed-off system. If you're building, this is your runbook.

What this hub covers

Every step between contract signing and partner handoff lives here. The sales rep gets the client. The build team delivers a launched, working system. The partner takes over for the long run. If you don't know what to do next, the answer is in this hub.

The build, in eight beats

Day 0
Contract signed in DocuSign

50% setup invoice goes out same day. Build clock starts the moment it's paid.

Day 1–2
Onboarding kickoff call

60 min. Set expectations, hand over asset checklist, lock launch date. (Section 02.)

Day 2–7
Asset collection window

Client delivers logo, photos, services, hours, FAQs. Build clock pauses on missing assets. (Section 03.)

Day 5–14
Site build

Template, copy, mobile QA, AEO/FAQ structure. (Section 04.)

Day 10–18
Voice agent + workflow agents + testing

Voice agent (Sophia) configuration, intake script, booking flow. Workflow agent builds — clone the GHL templates the client bought (Quote Follow-Up, No-Show Killer, etc.) into their sub-account. Test all agents against scenarios. (Sections 05–07.)

Day 14–20
Integrations

Phone provisioning, calendar, CRM, GHL workflows. (Section 08.)

Day 18–22
Launch

Pre-launch QA. Domain switch. First production call test. Client final approval. (Section 09.)

Day 22–52
Partner handoff & QC window

Knowledge transfer to partner. 30-day quality control window before full hand-off. (Sections 10–11.)

The Sons Test

Every page in this hub passes one test: could a smart 27-year-old build a launched client system from scratch using only this hub? If a step requires "ask Tom," it gets rewritten until it doesn't.

If the build goes sideways, the answer is here. If it isn't, flag it — and the hub gets updated.

Section 02

Client Onboarding.

The 60-minute kickoff call sets the tone for the whole build. Done well, it cuts asset-collection time in half and pre-empts 80% of "wait, I thought you were going to..." moments.

Same day as contract signing

  • Send the welcome email (template in Section 14). Confirms the kickoff call, attaches the asset checklist, sets expectations on the build clock.
  • Send the 50% setup invoice. Build clock does not start until paid.
  • Add client to CRM with all assessment data, discovery intake form, and the proposal copy.
  • Schedule the kickoff call within 48 hours of contract signing.

The 60-minute kickoff agenda

0:00 — 0:10
Welcome & expectations

Confirm: the build packet (PDF), the launch date, the partner-handoff structure, who their day-to-day contact is during build.

0:10 — 0:25
Stakeholder map

Who decides? Who provides assets? Who tests? Who signs off on launch? Get names and emails for each.

0:25 — 0:40
Asset checklist walk-through

Open the asset checklist (Section 03). Confirm a delivery date for each item. Identify which assets will be hardest to gather.

0:40 — 0:50
Sophia voice tuning

Get a sense of the brand voice. Formal? Warm? Get one or two voice samples (recordings of the owner talking to a customer if available).

0:50 — 1:00
Launch date lock + next steps

Confirm launch target. Set next check-in (Day 7). Send recap email with action items.

The build packet (PDF, sent at kickoff)

  • Build timeline with key dates
  • Asset checklist with deadlines
  • Builder contact info + business-hours response window
  • Partner handoff structure (Partner-Served clients only)
  • What's in scope vs. what's an upgrade
  • How change requests work after launch (handled by partner)
If they push past business hours

If a client expects you to be on call evenings/weekends, fix the expectation now. Builder support is business hours. Emergencies post-launch route to partner. "I'm not the right person for late-night fixes — that's what your partner Care plan is for."

Section 03

Asset Collection.

Builds slip when assets slip. The fix is a hard checklist with hard deadlines and a paused-clock rule.

The asset checklist (sent at kickoff)

Brand & visuals

  • Logo (PNG + SVG if available, transparent background)
  • Brand color codes (hex) or existing site reference
  • 10–20 photos (work, team, location). High-res, no stock.
  • Existing site URL / login (if applicable)

Business basics

  • Legal business name + DBA
  • Service areas (cities, ZIPs, radius)
  • Hours (with after-hours/emergency policy)
  • Phone, address, GBP listing URL
  • License numbers, certifications, awards

Services & pricing

  • Service list (categories + descriptions)
  • Pricing approach (flat / range / quote)
  • Specialties / niche services
  • What they explicitly do NOT do

FAQs & voice

  • Top 10 questions customers actually ask
  • How they typically answer each (their words)
  • Voice sample (recording of owner on a real call, if available)
  • Tone preference (formal / warm / direct)

Where it goes

Each client gets a folder in the shared drive: /clients/[business-name]/ with subfolders for brand/, photos/, copy/, sophia/, contracts/. Nothing lives in email.

The paused-clock rule

If assets are late

If a client misses an asset deadline by 48 hours, the build clock pauses. The launch date moves out by the same amount of time. "Build clock paused as of [date]. We'll restart the moment we receive [missing items]." Send by email. Document in CRM. Don't absorb the slip silently.

Asset receipt protocol

  • Confirm receipt within 4 business hours
  • Flag missing items immediately, in writing
  • If a photo is unusable (low res, watermarked), say so — don't try to make it work
  • Do not ask the client to "send a few more photos when you get a chance" — ask for specific shots with deadlines
Section 04

Site Build.

Build environment, template selection, copy, mobile QA, AEO/FAQ structure. No surprises — every build follows the same skeleton.

Template selection by package

PackageApproachSkeleton
Smart Site RefreshKeep existing structure. Rebuild copy and lead-capture surface.5 sections: hero, services, about, lead form, footer.
Smart Site + SophiaNew site or focused rebuild. Sophia-aware design.7 sections: hero, services, areas, why us, FAQ, lead form, footer.
Full Response SystemFull rebuild. Complete information architecture.10+ sections: hero, services (per-service pages), locations, about, team, reviews, FAQ, contact, blog/AEO, footer.

Copy guidelines

  • Lead with the customer's job. Not "We provide quality plumbing." → "Slow drain? We're 22 minutes away."
  • Specific over generic. "$0 dispatch fee, weekends included" beats "competitive pricing."
  • Service area in headlines. Local proof = trust + local SEO.
  • Real photos, real reviews. No stock images of strangers in white coats.
  • One CTA per fold. Call, book, or contact — pick one for each section.
  • Avoid jargon. If the owner wouldn't say it on a job site, don't put it on the page.

Mobile-first checklist

  • Tap targets at least 48×48px
  • Click-to-call button visible above the fold on mobile
  • Forms have one input per row on mobile
  • Hero image < 200KB after compression
  • Lighthouse mobile performance score 80+
  • No horizontal scroll on any breakpoint

AEO / FAQ structure

Every site ships with an FAQ section structured for answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews). Question as a real question. Answer in 1–3 sentences. Nothing else.

  • Service-specific FAQ on each service page (5–7 Qs)
  • Site-wide FAQ on /faq with 15–25 Qs
  • Schema markup: FAQPage JSON-LD on every FAQ section
  • Service area FAQs ("Do you serve [city]?") with specific answers

Pre-launch site QA

  • All forms submit and route to test inbox
  • All click-to-call buttons dial the right number
  • Sitemap + robots.txt deployed
  • Google Analytics + GBP linked
  • Contact info matches GBP exactly (NAP consistency)
  • No 404s on any internal link
  • Mobile + desktop visual review on 3 real devices
  • Open Graph image + meta description set per page
Section 05

Sophia Configuration.

Voice, intake script, booking flow, lead routing. Sophia is configured per-client — the template is the floor, not the ceiling.

Voice tuning

  • Voice selection matches the brand. Warm-female default for most service businesses. Match accent to region where reasonable.
  • Pace 1.0× default. Slow to 0.95× for older customer base, never faster than 1.05×.
  • Greeting includes business name + a single qualifying signal: "Hi, you've reached [Business]. Thanks for calling. Are you calling about a new job, or about an existing job we're already working on?"

The intake script (template)

5-question Sophia intake (default)
  • Q1 — Trade-specific lead qualifier. Plumbing: "What's going on with your plumbing?" Locksmith: "What kind of lock issue?" HVAC: "Heating, cooling, or both?"
  • Q2 — Address / service area. "What's the address so I can let our team know where to go?"
  • Q3 — Urgency. "Is this an emergency, or can it wait until our next available slot?"
  • Q4 — Best contact. "What's the best phone number for our team to reach you?"
  • Q5 — Booking. Read available slots from calendar. "I have [time A] or [time B] open. Which works?"

Booking flow

  • Calendar source: Calendly (default) or GHL calendar (if integrated). One source of truth.
  • Slot windows: Match owner's defined hours. Emergency window (24/7) routes differently — see Q3.
  • Confirmation: SMS to customer + email to owner with full transcript and booking details, within 60 seconds of hangup.
  • Failure mode: If Sophia can't book (no slots, customer drops off, etc.), the call summary still goes to the owner with the lead info captured.

Lead routing

  • Standard call → calendar booking + summary email/SMS to owner
  • Emergency call → immediate SMS to owner's emergency line + booking attempt
  • Existing customer / not-a-lead → message taken, summary to owner, no calendar booking
  • Spam / wrong number → discarded, no notification
Hard rule

Sophia's intake script is configured to be transparent and professional. She does not deny being an AI agent if directly asked. The product is "the call gets handled and the booking happens" — not "the customer thinks she's human."

Section 06

Workflow Agent Builds.

Build steps for the non-voice agents in the catalog. Voice agent (Vapi) lives in Section 05. This section covers the GHL workflow agents and the on-site chat / lead-qualification agents. Each card maps a Sales Hub catalog entry to its actual build process in GHL.

Before you start any workflow build

Confirm the client has a GHL sub-account provisioned (Section 08 covers GHL setup). Confirm whether the agent needs SMS — if yes, 10DLC is submitted Day 1 of build (1–2 weeks for carrier approval). All workflow agents below are cloned from the master template sub-account into the client's sub-account — never built from scratch.

Chat & on-site agents

01 · Website Chat Agent

Catalog entry: Sales Hub Section 04, Agent #02. Standalone $1,500 + Care.

Inputs needed from client: 10–15 most-common customer questions with their preferred answers (in their voice); business hours; click-to-call number; lead routing preference (email or GHL contact).

Build steps:

  1. Configure the chat agent in GHL (Conversations → Bot setup) with the client's FAQ + answers.
  2. Set lead-capture form: name, contact, reason for contact. No medical free-text on white coat builds (per Sales Hub Section 03 Path B rule).
  3. Generate the embed snippet.
  4. Get CMS access from client OR email them the snippet with paste-before-</body> instructions.
  5. Test on mobile and desktop in production after embed.

Test cases: Q with exact-match in FAQ → agent answers correctly. Q outside FAQ → agent captures lead and offers human follow-up. Mobile responsiveness on 3 real devices. No console errors on client's site.

Handoff to partner: CMS login (in shared credential vault), GHL bot config export, FAQ source doc, list of common edge-case Qs the partner should review monthly.

02 · Lead Qualification Agent

Catalog entry: Sales Hub Section 04, Agent #03. Standalone $2,500 + Care.

Inputs needed from client: Qualifying criteria (scope, location, urgency, budget thresholds); ideal-fit profile; what to do with unqualified leads (auto-reply with referral, or just decline politely).

Build steps:

  1. Define the 3–5 qualifying questions (scope-of-work, service area, urgency, budget).
  2. Configure scoring: which answers route to booking, which route to "polite decline" path.
  3. Build the GHL workflow: trigger on inbound lead → qualifying questions (SMS, chat, or voice) → branch on answers → route to calendar OR park with a polite-decline message.
  4. Connect to the client's calendar/CRM.

Test cases: Send a qualifying lead through — should book. Send an unqualified lead — should decline politely. Edge case: lead answers ambiguously — should fall back to human.

Handoff to partner: Workflow export, qualifying criteria source doc (so the partner can adjust as the client's market shifts).

GHL workflow agents

All built by cloning from the master template sub-account into the client's sub-account. The original templates live in Tom's master sub-account. Don't rebuild from scratch — clone, customize, deploy.

03 · Quote Follow-Up

Catalog entry: Sales Hub Section 04, Agent #04. Standalone $1,500 + Care. GHL template: "Follow-Up 1: Quoted, Not Booked."

Inputs needed from client: What constitutes a "quote sent" event in their CRM; the email signature/voice they want for the follow-up sequence.

Build steps:

  1. Clone "Follow-Up 1: Quoted, Not Booked" template from master sub-account into client's sub-account.
  2. Customize the 3 email touches (Day 2, Day 5, Day 10) with client's voice, services, and contact details.
  3. If SMS upgrade: customize the matching SMS touches (requires 10DLC).
  4. Set trigger: tag added to contact when quote sent (via CRM integration or manual tag).
  5. Set exit conditions: contact books appointment, replies to email, or sequence completes.

Test cases: Tag a test contact → emails fire on schedule. Reply to an email → sequence stops. Book an appointment → sequence stops.

Handoff to partner: Workflow export, copy doc with the 3 email templates, list of required tags in CRM.

04 · Annual Service Reminder

Catalog entry: Sales Hub Section 04, Agent #05. Standalone $1,500 + Care. GHL template: "Follow-Up 2: Annual Service Due."

Inputs needed from client: Service cycle length (HVAC: 12 months, dental: 6 months, gutter: 6 months, etc.); date field in CRM that marks "last service date"; copy variants for each service type.

Build steps:

  1. Clone "Follow-Up 2: Annual Service Due" template.
  2. Set the trigger date math: last_service_date + cycle_months − 4 weeks = first reminder fires.
  3. Customize 2–3 reminder touches (4 weeks out, 2 weeks out, 1 week out).
  4. Wire booking link to client's calendar.

Test cases: Backdate a test contact's service date → verify reminders fire on schedule. Click the booking link → lands on the right calendar.

Handoff to partner: Workflow export, cycle-month rule doc, copy doc.

05 · Reactivation Caller

Catalog entry: Sales Hub Section 04, Agent #06. Standalone $1,500 + Care. GHL template: "Follow-Up 3: Reactivation."

Inputs needed from client: Definition of "cold" (12 months no contact? 18 months? 24?); reactivation offer copy (discount, free check-up, etc., if any); CRM segment that contains the cold list.

Build steps:

  1. Clone "Follow-Up 3: Reactivation" template.
  2. Customize: 3-touch sequence (Touch 1: "we miss you" friendly, Touch 2: offer or reminder, Touch 3: last call).
  3. Wire the trigger: contact tag cold-12mo (or whatever cold rule applies) added.
  4. Build the cold-segment query in GHL contacts.
  5. Schedule the segment to run quarterly (or as client prefers) to refresh the cold list.

Test cases: Tag a test contact → sequence fires. Verify deliverability (spam check the email content).

Handoff to partner: Workflow export, cold-segment query doc, copy doc.

06 · No-Show Killer

Catalog entry: Sales Hub Section 04, Agent #07. Standalone $1,500 + Care. GHL template: "No Show Killer." Requires 10DLC SMS approval.

Inputs needed from client: Calendar/booking source; "appointment created" trigger details; preferred SMS reminder voice.

Build steps:

  1. Submit 10DLC application Day 1 of build (1–2 weeks for approval).
  2. Clone "No Show Killer" template.
  3. Wire the trigger to fire on "appointment created" event in client's calendar.
  4. Build the SMS sequence: 24h before (reminder + confirm), 2h before (final reminder + map link), post-appointment (thank-you + review-ask handoff).

Section 07

Agent Testing.

No agent ships before sign-off. Voice agent (Sophia) must pass the five test scenarios below. Workflow agents must pass the per-agent test cases listed in Section 06 (Workflow Agent Builds). Both stages are recorded, reviewed, and signed off in writing before launch.

Two stages of testing

Voice agent testing (this section, below): five live-call scenarios for Sophia. Workflow agent testing: each Section 06 agent card includes its own test cases — trigger the workflow with a test contact, verify the sequence fires correctly, verify exit conditions, verify deliverability. Both stages must complete before the launch checklist (Section 09) can be opened.

Voice agent — five required test scenarios

Test 1 · Happy path
Standard new lead, business hours

Customer with a typical issue, calls during open hours, books a regular slot. Sophia should: greet, qualify, capture address, capture phone, book, confirm. Total time under 4 minutes.

Test 2 · Emergency
After-hours emergency

Customer with an urgent issue at 11pm. Sophia should: recognize emergency cue, capture lead info quickly, trigger emergency SMS to owner, give customer realistic next-step expectation.

Test 3 · Existing customer
Calling about a job in progress

Customer says "I'm calling about the job at 123 Maple, the technician was supposed to come back today." Sophia should: not try to book a new appointment, capture message, route to owner without calendar action.

Test 4 · Confused / unclear
Customer rambles or is unclear

Customer talks for 90 seconds without clearly stating the issue. Sophia should: politely interrupt, restate to confirm understanding, re-ask Q1.

Test 5 · Out-of-area
Lead outside service area

Customer in a city the business doesn't serve. Sophia should: confirm address early, politely decline, end call cleanly — not book a job that can't be served.

Sign-off criteria (all 5 must pass)

  • Sophia greeted with correct business name
  • Captured address accurately
  • Captured phone number accurately (read back to customer)
  • Routed to correct outcome (booking / message / decline)
  • Summary email arrived within 60 seconds with full transcript
  • SMS confirmation went to customer if booked
  • No false "I'm a real person" claims if asked directly
  • Total call time under 5 minutes for happy path

Common failure modes

  • Sophia can't hear address numbers clearly. Fix: tune phone number/audio routing, slow read-back pace.
  • Sophia books outside business hours. Fix: tighten calendar availability, double-check time-zone settings.
  • Sophia doesn't end gracefully. Fix: add explicit close prompt; default to "Anything else I can help with? — Have a great day."
  • Customer interrupts and Sophia talks over. Fix: enable barge-in handling in voice config.
No launch without sign-off

If Sophia fails any of the five, she does not launch. The launch date moves. Tell the client immediately. Never launch a Sophia who can't handle Test 2 (emergency) cleanly — that's the call that pays for the system.

Section 08

Integrations.

Phone provisioning, calendar sync, CRM connection, GHL workflows. The plumbing that makes Sophia's outputs land in the right places.

Phone number provisioning

  • Default: provision a new local number via Twilio matching the business's area code.
  • Forwarding: client's existing main line forwards to the Twilio number after-hours (or 24/7, depending on package).
  • SMS-capable: all numbers must be 10DLC-registered before SMS confirmations go out. Plan for 1–2 weeks for 10DLC approval.
  • Ownership: the number stays in our Twilio account during service. On cancellation, we port it to the client (or partner) at cost.

CRM integration

Most service-business clients are on one of: ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro, or "nothing structured." Default play:

  • If they have a CRM: push Sophia leads into a "Sophia Inbound" stage. Don't auto-merge with existing customers — let the owner approve.
  • If they don't: send leads to GHL (our managed CRM). Set up the basic pipeline.
  • Reporting: weekly digest email with new leads, booked jobs, lost calls.

Section 09

Launch Checklist.

Go-live runbook. Run it in this order. Don't skip steps because they "feel done."

T-minus 7 days

  • Site QA complete (Section 04 checklist)
  • Agent testing complete & signed off (Section 07)
  • 10DLC SMS approved (if applicable)
  • All integrations tested in production mode
  • Final invoice sent (50% on launch, due before launch)
  • Client preview link sent for sign-off

T-minus 48 hours

  • Final client sign-off received in writing
  • Launch invoice paid
  • Partner pre-handoff briefing complete (Partner-Served only)
  • Client notified of exact go-live time

Launch day

  • DNS / domain switch executed
  • Old site retired (or 301 redirected)
  • Phone forwarding activated
  • First production call test — live, real number, real Sophia
  • SMS confirmation tested end-to-end
  • Email summary tested end-to-end
  • GBP profile updated with new site URL (if changed)
  • Launch announcement email to client (with first-week monitoring schedule)

T-plus 24 hours

  • Verify any inbound calls handled correctly
  • Check error logs / failed integrations
  • Send client end-of-day-1 status report
  • Begin partner handoff documentation
Never launch on Friday

Default launch days: Tuesday or Wednesday. Avoids the "something broke at 5pm Friday and now it's broken all weekend" trap. The first 48 hours need attention.

Delivered to the partner within 5 business days of launch. Stored in /clients/[business-name]/handoff/. Lives forever.

Build artifacts

  • Site source / CMS access
  • Sophia configuration export
  • Twilio number + account access (read-only)
  • Calendar / CRM credentials (read-write for partner)
  • GHL workflow exports
  • Domain / DNS records

Operating docs

  • Client-specific Sophia notes (voice tuning, edge cases)
  • Service-area + pricing reference
  • Stakeholder map (who decides what, who pays)
  • Active integrations list
  • Known issues / quirks discovered during build

Partner kickoff meeting

30 minutes, scheduled within 3 days of launch. Builder + partner + Tom (oversight). Agenda:

  • Walk-through of handoff package
  • Live demo of the system in production
  • Partner asks questions; builder answers
  • Confirm partner's first client touchpoint date (within 7 days of launch)
  • Confirm escalation rules (Section 11)

Sent by builder + cc partner, within 24 hours of partner kickoff. Template:

White Coat Path A — the BAA gate

Do not start building without it

For Path A (HIPAA-touching) builds, the partner signs the BAA with the practice before any build work begins. The build phase uses synthetic data only — no PHI is created, received, or accessed by Point Zero AI at any point. Real-data activation happens post-launch by the partner under the BAA. If the BAA isn't in place at kickoff, the build doesn't start. Tom does not sign the BAA. Point Zero AI does not sign the BAA. No exceptions.

Tom-Direct clients (the 10) — modified handoff

For Tom-Direct clients, the system stays under Tom for support. The handoff package still gets created (so a future move to partner is possible), but the partner kickoff meeting is skipped. Tom is the named support contact in the client introduction email.

  • Flag any "Sophia did something weird" patterns

Escalation rules

Most issues post-launch route to partner. A short list comes back to builder + Tom:

  • Customer complaint about Sophia's accuracy → partner handles. Loops in builder if pattern emerges (3+ in a week).
  • System outage > 30 minutes → partner notifies builder + Tom immediately.
  • Client asks for scope expansion → partner refers to builder; builder writes upgrade quote (not free).
  • Client threatens cancellation → partner notifies Tom directly within 24 hours.
  • Legal / compliance issue surfaces → partner notifies Tom immediately. Builder pauses any related work.
  • Partner not delivering → Tom escalates to partner principal. Replacement process in Partner Hub.
The handoff is real

After Day 30, builder is out unless escalated. The point of the partner model is that the builder doesn't become the long-tail support. If Tom or the builder is still doing weekly client work past Day 30, the handoff failed.

Section 12

Never Say.

Language that creates legal, support, or expectation traps. If a builder writes any of these in an email, slack, or contract, edit before sending.

Sophia & AI transparency

  • "The customer doesn't know it's AI." → "Sophia is configured to be transparent and professional."
  • "She'll fool anyone." → "She handles the intake cleanly; the booking is what matters."
  • "We'll make her sound human." → "The voice is natural and clear; transparency about what she is, when asked."

HIPAA / compliance

  • "We sign a BAA." → "Our HIPAA-capable support partner signs the BAA directly with the practice before any PHI-touching work begins."
  • "HIPAA-certified" anything. → Use "HIPAA-capable" or "operating under a signed BAA."
  • "All our agents are HIPAA-compliant." → "PHI-touching workflows route to a partner-led Path A build with BAA in place."
  • "Don't worry about compliance, we'll handle it." → never. Compliance has owners, in writing, with contracts.
  • "It's HIPAA-ready out of the box." → never. HIPAA is not a feature flag; it's a contractual structure.

Scope & support

  • "Sure, we can add that." → "That's an upgrade — let me get you a scope and quote."
  • "Don't worry, we'll figure it out." → "Let me document this and come back with a clear plan tomorrow."
  • "You can call me anytime." → "Business hours support during build; ongoing support is your partner Care plan."
  • "Lifetime support included." → never. Care is month-to-month, post-launch.
  • "Unlimited revisions." → never. Every Care tier has change-volume bands.

Promises about results

  • "Sophia will pay for herself in month one." → "Most clients see the system pay for itself within the first month from a single booked job — but it depends on volume and conversion."
  • "Guaranteed leads." → never. We deliver a system; conversion is downstream of many factors.
  • "You'll book more jobs." → "You'll capture every after-hours and missed-call lead, which is the difference most clients see."
Section 13

Never Build.

Features and configurations that are off the table, regardless of how much the client wants them. The Red Zone for builders.

PHI & HIPAA-adjacent (Path B clients)

  • No PHI collection in any form, intake, or Sophia flow on a Path B build.
  • No insurance verification agent or workflow.
  • No patient recall agent.
  • No "is this an emergency / is this serious" medical-triage logic.
  • No diagnosis-adjacent intake ("describe your symptoms" type flows).
  • No file uploads of medical records through any form we build.

Regulated advice

  • No legal advice agents (contract review, document drafting, "is this enforceable")
  • No financial advice agents (investment recs, tax advice, securities-adjacent)
  • No medical diagnosis or triage agents
  • No insurance claims advice agents
  • No mental-health or crisis intervention agents

Scope & ergonomics

  • No 24/7 builder availability promise. Build hours are business hours.
  • No "we'll integrate with [obscure vendor]" without scope and quote first.
  • No dropping site features at the client's last-minute request when those features are why the system works.
  • No multi-location builds on standard pricing — that's Custom (Section 03 of Sales Hub).
  • No "white label this so it looks like a different company" — we're building Point Zero AI systems.

Client behaviors that pause the build

  • Client misses asset deadline by 48+ hours → build clock pauses, launch slips.
  • Client demands launch on Friday → politely decline. Reschedule.
  • Client asks for scope expansion mid-build → document, quote, change order. Build does not absorb new scope silently.
  • Client refuses agent testing sign-off → no launch. Document, escalate to Tom.
When in doubt

If a build request makes you reach for a creative workaround — that's the moment to check this list. The boundary exists precisely because reasonable-sounding asks become bad businesses one yes at a time.
